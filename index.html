FLIP MECHANISM — HOW IT WORKS
The platform is a section of the table top on a central horizontal pivot axis. When stored (0°): miter saw hangs below table, table top is completely flat. Hit FLIP: platform rotates 180° around its center — saw comes above surface. Table "hole" is always the same piece of wood, just flipped.
COPLANARITY — CRITICAL
Lay a 48" straightedge across: table saw surface → outfeed strips → flip platform top. Zero gap or rock. Shim table saw support shelf with lag-bolt levelers until all at 34.5".
PIVOT ALIGNMENT
Shoulder bolt axis must be at Z=33.75" (table surface minus 0.75" — the midpoint of the 1.5" thick platform). Off by 1/8" and the stored surface won't be flush.
BUILD SEQUENCE
1) Frame + legs. 2) Bottom rails. 3) Top rails. 4) Back panel. 5) Support shelves. 6) Table saw cutout + install saw (shim flush). 7) Top MDF panels. 8) Flip platform + pivot hardware. 9) Drawers. 10) Install casters last.
DUST COLLECTION
3" hole in back panel at Z=26" behind table saw. Route 2.5" hose to cyclone lid on vac in right lower bay. Miter saw 1.5" port via adaptor to same cyclone.
